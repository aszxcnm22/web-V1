Research Summary: An algorithm for variational inclusion problems including quasi-nonexpansive mappings with applications in osteoporosis prediction

This article presents a novel algorithmic process for solving fixed-point problems for quasi-nonexpansive mappings and variational inclusion problems within real Hilbert spaces. The proposed method demonstrates weak convergence under suitable assumptions. Additionally, we apply this algorithm to data classification for predicting osteoporosis risk using Extreme Learning Machine (ELM). Experimental results show that our proposed algorithm outperforms existing algorithms across multiple evaluation metrics. Notably, it achieves higher accuracy, precision, and F1-score across various training scenarios compared to other methods. The area under the curve (AUC) from the receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curve further confirms the efficiency of our approach, highlighting its generalizability and superior classification performance. These results emphasize the effectiveness and robustness of our proposed algorithm, demonstrating its potential for advancing osteoporosis risk prediction through improved inclusion and classification capabilities.

Application to Osteoporosis Risk Prediction

Importance of Osteoporosis

  • Osteoporosis is a global health issue, especially among the elderly.
  • It weakens bones and increases the risk of fractures.
  • This condition can lead to complications, loss of mobility, and a higher mortality rate.

Role of Machine Learning in Osteoporosis Analysis

  • Machine Learning (ML) can analyze demographic data, lifestyle, genetics, and bone density.
  • It helps identify high-risk individuals at an early stage.
  • This enables early intervention and prevention of complications caused by osteoporosis.

Developing a New Algorithm for Enhanced Prediction Accuracy

  • A newly developed algorithm is used as an optimizer for Extreme Learning Machine (ELM).
  • The dataset from Kaggle, covering demographics, lifestyle, and medical history, is utilized.
  • This improves accuracy in analyzing and predicting osteoporosis risk.

Risk Factors Associated with Osteoporosis

  • Age: Natural bone deterioration increases with age.
  • Hormonal Changes: Such as menopause, which reduces estrogen levels.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Especially calcium and vitamin D.
  • Lifestyle Factors: Lack of exercise or an unhealthy diet.

Diagnosis and Prevention Strategies

  • Diagnosis is performed using DEXA Scan, which measures bone mass density (T-score < -2.5 indicates osteoporosis).
  • Exercise, proper intake of calcium and vitamin D, and lifestyle adjustments help reduce risk.

Benefits of Using Machine Learning in Osteoporosis Management

  • Enables faster risk prediction.
  • Helps doctors and medical personnel develop appropriate treatment plans.
  • Increases the chance of prevention and reduces the burden on public health systems.

    Explanation

      The illustration demonstrates the 5-fold cross-validation technique, used to evaluate machine learning model performance by dividing the dataset into five equal parts (folds).

      Each Fold:

    • In each iteration, the data is split into two parts:
    • Training Data: 80% of the total data (purple) is used for model training.
    • Validation Data: 20% of the total data (green) is used for evaluating model performance.
    • Each fold uses different subsets of data for validation to ensure that the entire dataset is utilized for validation at least once.

      • Process:

    • This process is repeated five times, with a different fold used for validation in each iteration while the remaining folds are used for training.
    • The results from each fold are averaged to assess the model‚Äôs overall performance.

      • Benefits:

    • 5-fold cross-validation helps reduce overfitting (where a model learns the training data too well and performs poorly on new data).
    • Provides a more reliable evaluation of model performance by using the entire dataset for validation.

Conclusions

We proposed a new algorithm (Algorithm 4) to address variable inclusion and fixed-point issues associated with semi-expansive transformations in real Hilbert spaces. Our main theorem establishes the weak convergence of the proposed algorithm under certain conditions. Additionally, we applied the algorithm in extreme learning machines for classification problems, particularly predicting osteoporosis risk. Our algorithm achieved over 82% accuracy, over 91% precision, 76% recall, and over 83% F1-score across all training boxes, demonstrating its effectiveness.
